Ways To Prevent Fire in your Home






Brief Background

In the Philippines, March is recognized as Fire Prevention Month, making it the ideal time to talk about and spread knowledge of the causes, risks, and dangers of fire. According to the proposal, the fire prevention month campaign encourages "safety consciousness among our people on a year-round basis as a positive preventive approach to a problem that may be remedied by increased caution, vigilance, sobriety, the use of common sense, and respect for the law."


Ways to Prevent Fire

1. While cooking, never leave the kitchen unattended.


The time it takes for a fire to start is really short. Get someone to watch the food for you if you have to leave the kitchen for any reason at all, or simply turn everything off until you return.


2. Examine your heating systems.


Your home's heating sources may very possibly start a house fire if they aren't operating properly. Always change the filters in your air conditioner, keep your heaters away from anything that could catch fire, and have a professional inspect these things once a year.

3. Any combustible items should be stored safely.


If exposed to a significant source of heat, household cleansers and products like hair spray or shaving cream may become quite dangerous. Always keep these safely locked up and away from heaters in a cool location.

4. Keep your cables clean.


Try to be considerate and check the wire for damage or chewing before plugging anything into a socket. Broken wires should be replaced as soon as possible because they can provide a risk for a fire. Moreover, keep in mind that when in use, wires can get hot, so pay attention to where you lay them and avoid passing them through carpets or other potentially flammable materials.

5. Be careful around open flames.


You may even consider lighting the fireplace to make your home feel more cozier on a chilly winter night. We won't advise you to completely stop using candles and fireplaces, but we will advise you to exercise caution. Turn off all of your candles before dozing off, and if you have pets, try to keep the open flames as far away from them as you can.



6. Keep a blanket or fire extinguisher nearby at all times!


Your best line of defense in the case of a fire starting will be blankets and fire extinguishers. To ensure that anything that does catch fire in your home can be extinguished as quickly as possible, everyone in the family should be familiar with how to use a fire extinguisher. Fire blankets are also excellent for extinguishing little fires before they get larger and more furious.
